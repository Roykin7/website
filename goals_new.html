1. EVO PROJECT - Digital Skills for the Future Project
Overview
The "Digital Skills for the Future" project is bridging the digital skills gap for Ugandan secondary school students by providing comprehensive, hands-on digital training. Targeting S1 to S6 students across urban and rural areas, the program fosters digital proficiency to empower youth for Uganda's digital economy.
How It Works
  • Dynamic curriculum delivered over six weekends per term
  • Topics: Programming, Graphic Design, Digital Citizenship, AI, IoT, and more
  • Students build personal project portfolios to showcase their skills
  • Teachers receive professional development for sustainable digital education

2. Digital Workplace
Digital workplace leaders face a complex web of challenges: optimizing costs, delivering foundational productivity tools, improving digital employee experience, enabling hybrid work and deploying everyday AI. To succeed in these objectives, digital workplace leaders must set a digital workplace application strategy that guides application selection and transforms the digital employee experience.
The Digital Workplace initiative seeks to transform workplaces in Uganda and East Africa by deploying STEM-driven digital solutions to address low digital maturity, infrastructure gaps, and gender disparities. Targeting small and medium enterprises (SMEs), public institutions, and youth/women-led organizations, the project enhances productivity, enables hybrid work, and integrates generative AI to improve digital employee experiences (DEX).
Key Focus Areas
  • STEM-driven digital transformation solutions
  • Bridging infrastructure and skills gaps
  • Promoting gender inclusivity in tech adoption
  • Enhancing productivity through AI integration
  • Supporting hybrid work environments
3. Tujulize Uganda: Empowering Citizens Through Digital Governance
Tujulize Uganda is an innovative platform designed to bridge the gap between citizens and local governance hence increasing their knowledge levels. By leveraging accessible technology, it empowers Ugandans to report service gaps, track district budgets, and engage in governance regardless of their technical skills or location.
The platform addresses unique challenges faced by different user groups - from rural farmers with basic phones to tech-savvy urban youth activists. Through culturally sensitive solutions, Tujulize Uganda counters disinformation, protects digital rights, and fosters an informed, resilient citizenry even during internet disruptions.
Platform Features
  • Accessible technology for all user groups
  • Service gap reporting and tracking
  • District budget transparency tools
  • Civic engagement facilitation
  • Digital rights protection
  • Disinformation counter-measures
